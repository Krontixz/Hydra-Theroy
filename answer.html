How This Becomes a World Record

To break the record for the biggest number, you don't just "pick a number." You define a function that produces one... RIGHT?

1. The Logic

We use something called Mathematical Induction. Our game proves that even if the Hydra grows faster than a computer can calculate, it must eventually hit zero. The total number of steps is our "Big Number."

2. Why it beats other numbers

Most big numbers use exponents (like 10 to the power of 10). But this game uses Recursion. Each step depends on the previous step's growth. This creates a "growth rate" that eventually overtakes Graham's Number.

3. How to solve it

  • Step A: Map the Hydra's heads to Ordinals (special math symbols like ε₀).
  • Step B: Prove that with every chop, the Ordinal gets smaller, even if the number of heads gets bigger.
  • Step C: Calculate the Maximum Bound of the sequence.

How we changed it

  • While the Hydra Game was proven in 1982, this website defines the Hashim Number, which applies the Hydra growth rule to a starting set of heads defined by the Busy Beaver function Σ(n). This creates a finite, computable number that exceeds Rayo's Number by shifting the logic from simple set theory to complex computability
